# squinch


## Noun

1. a small arch built across the interior angle of two walls (usually to support a spire)
	- Less specific
		- arch
	- More specific
		- trumpet arch

## Verb

1. crouch down
	- Less specific
		- crouch
		- stoop
		- bend
		- bow
2. draw back, as with fear or pain; “she flinched when they showed the slaughtering of the calf”
	- Synonyms
		- flinch
		- funk
		- cringe
		- shrink
		- wince
		- recoil
		- quail
	- Less specific
		- move
	- More specific
		- shrink back
		- retract
	- Related
		- wince
		- wince
		- flinch
		- wince
		- flinch
3. cross one’s eyes as if in strabismus; “The children squinted so as to scare each other”
	- Synonyms
		- squint
	- Less specific
		- grimace
		- make a face
		- pull a face
	- Related
		- squinter
		- squint-eye
		- squint
